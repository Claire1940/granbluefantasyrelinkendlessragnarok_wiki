<Section icon={Swords} title="What Changes in Combat, Roster, and Endgame">

The biggest difference is not raw combat controls. Both versions still revolve around action timing, party synergy, and repeatable quests. The real shift is that Endless Ragnarok adds more reasons to keep building characters after the base game curve settles down.

| Feature | Base Game | Endless Ragnarok | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| Playable characters | Large launch roster | Six new playable characters | More build variety and team options |
| Quest pressure | Story and postgame quest flow | New Chaos difficulty | Harder fights for stronger builds |
| Solo mode | Standard quest progression | Conflux solo mode | Extra endgame path for solo players |

| Boss pressure | Standard boss learning curve | Beelzebub, The World, and stronger challenge fights | More demanding mechanics and execution |

The new character pool is the most visible upgrade. Endless Ragnarok adds **Gallanza, Maglielle, Beatrix, Eustace, Fraux, and Fediel**, which is a major jump for players who like roster variety. The expansion also introduces **Summons**, **Chaos quests**, and **Conflux**, so the late game feels less like a repetition loop and more like a new progression lane.
