<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Build Priority and Progression">

AI teammates get better when you keep their builds simple and efficient. Do not chase every shiny upgrade at once. Strong AI performance usually comes from **good weapons, practical sigils, and a build that matches the character’s natural strengths**. That approach scales well from story quests into late-game farming.

<Callout type="tip" title="Best Build Mindset">
For AI, consistency beats cleverness. A reliable weapon path and a small set of high-value sigils usually outperform a messy “best in slot” pile with poor synergy.
</Callout>

| Progression Stage | Priority | AI-Friendly Focus |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Early game | Basic weapon upgrades | Simple damage, health, and cooldown value |
| Mid game | Role-based sigils | Better uptime, survivability, and utility |
| Late game | Damage Cap and core offense | Push real damage without overcomplicating the build |
| Endgame | Sigil Synthesis, Master Traits, hard quest tools | Refine strong setups instead of rebuilding from zero |

A practical progression path looks like this:

- **Upgrade the weapon** on your most-used AI characters first.
- **Use sigils that improve uptime** instead of niche stats the AI cannot exploit well.
- **Keep one support slot** for healing, buffs, or safety if your team feels fragile.
- **Invest harder in S-tier units** before spreading rare materials across the whole roster.
- **Revisit expansion systems** once you reach harder postgame content and need tighter clears.
