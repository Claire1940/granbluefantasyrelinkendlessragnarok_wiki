<Section icon={Target} title="Siegfried Combo Flow and Battle Rhythm">

The base combat loop for Siegfried is built around controlled aggression. The [battle manual](https://relink.granbluefantasy.jp/en/manual/detail?s=battles) explains the broader system well, but the practical rule for Siegfried is easy: build pressure with normal attacks, then cash in during safe windows.

You do not need to force maximum input speed. You need repeatable windows. That means you should recognize when a boss is idle, staggered, recovering, or locked into a long animation, then spend your heavier tools there.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Open with controlled pressure">
    Start with normal attack strings to establish rhythm. Do not burn all your tools immediately; your first job is to read the fight.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Use skills on real openings">
    Fire skills after a dodge, during a stagger, or when the boss finishes a long attack. This keeps your damage efficient instead of wasted.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Convert team momentum">
    When the party creates a Link Attack or a stun window, commit harder. This is where Siegfried can turn stable play into a real burst swing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Reset before danger returns">
    If the boss starts a dangerous phase, stop chasing one extra hit. Guard, dodge, and re-enter on the next clean opening.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Window | Best Action | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Boss recovery | Short attack string | Safe pressure without overcommitting |
| Stagger or stun | Skill burst | Higher damage in a protected window |
| Link Attack prompt | Join immediately | Better party damage and tempo |
| Chain Burst setup | Save Skybound Arts | Bigger team-wide payoff |
| Incoming danger | Guard or dodge | Preserves uptime for the next cycle |

<Callout type="warning" title="Common Mistake">
Do not treat Siegfried like a character who should always finish the longest possible string. On difficult fights, the best string is often the one that ends before the boss’s next swing.
</Callout>

</Section>
