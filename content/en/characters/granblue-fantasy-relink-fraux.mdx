<Section icon={Swords} title="Best Fraux Build Priorities">

Fraux does not need a complicated opening build. Start with weapon level and mastery, then move into sigils that improve real quest performance. When her damage starts flattening in practice, shift toward **Damage Cap** and the stronger endgame picks that matter in harder fights.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Stabilize the weapon first">
    Use your best available weapon, keep it upgraded, and make sure Fraux is not entering quests undergeared.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Cover the core loop">
    Add simple offense, cooldown help, and one or two comfort sigils so she can stay on the field longer.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Add true damage tools">
    When damage starts flattening, move into Damage Cap, then layer War Elemental or Supplementary Damage if available.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Tune for the mode">
    For long boss fights, favor survivability and consistency. For farm runs, trim comfort and keep pressure high.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Build Stage | What to Prioritize | What to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Early | Weapon level, basic offense, skill uptime | Spending rare mats on premium sigils too soon |
| Mid | Survivability, cooldown, steady damage | A full glass-cannon setup |
| Late | Damage Cap, War Elemental, Supplementary Damage | Ignoring combat consistency |
| Expansion | Master Traits, refined traits, tough-quest tuning | Copying another character's exact build |

| Sigil Type | Value for Fraux | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| Attack / HP / Defense | Good | Early comfort and smoother clears |
| Cooldown help | Good | Keeps rotations and skill loops stable |
| Damage Cap | Excellent | Late-game scaling when raw attack slows down |
| War Elemental | Excellent | Premium damage against tougher targets |
| Supplementary Damage | Excellent | Adds value once the build is already stable |

<Callout type="warning" title="Upgrade Priority">
