<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Loop, Party Synergy, and Common Mistakes">

Tweyen performs best when the team creates space for her. She does not need to be the front line; she needs openings. That means your job is to keep firing when the boss is busy, then reset cleanly when the fight becomes unsafe.

| Situation | Best Response | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| Boss is stationary | Fire your strongest attacks | Highest uptime window |
| Boss starts moving | Reposition early | Keep damage flowing |
| Team triggers a stun window | Spend burst skills | Maximize party damage |
| Link Attack appears | Join immediately | Stack team value fast |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Open from a safe angle">
    Start fights from range and keep your camera discipline clean. Tweyen gains value when you avoid chasing the boss into bad positions.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Spend damage during openings">
    Use your stronger attacks when the boss is locked into an animation, stunned, or recovering from a big move.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Reset instead of overcommitting">
    If the boss starts a dangerous pattern, stop forcing damage. Reposition first, then resume pressure.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Chain with the team">
    Follow Link Attacks and Chain Burst opportunities. Tweyen becomes much better when the whole party attacks in sync.
  </Step>
</Steps>
