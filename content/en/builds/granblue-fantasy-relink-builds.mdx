<Section icon={Swords} title="Early Game Setup: The Fastest Way to Stay Strong">

The safest opening route is to lock in one character you enjoy and keep that character slightly ahead of the rest of the party. The base game’s four-person team structure rewards consistency more than random gear swapping.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Pick one main character">
    Choose a character with a comfortable rhythm, then keep that unit ahead in weapon level, skills, and sigils.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Use only obvious upgrades">
    Spend early materials on clear power spikes: weapon level, basic offense, HP, and comfort stats.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Let AI fill the gaps">
    Build your party so AI allies cover range, defense, healing, or utility that your main character lacks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Learn the battle loop">
    Focus on dodges, guard timing, skill windows, Link Attacks, and Chain Bursts before chasing advanced optimization.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Save rare materials">
    Hold back premium resources until you know which characters will stay in your long-term roster.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Resource | Spend it on | Hold it for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Weapons | Your active party | Bench characters |
| Mastery Points | Core damage and survivability | Tiny stat nodes |
| Sigils | Common practical stats | Perfect endgame rolls |
| Rare mats | Real power spikes | Backup builds |

<Callout type="warning" title="Early Mistake to Avoid">
Do not fully invest in every weapon you unlock. That habit slows progression and leaves your best character underpowered when quest difficulty rises.
</Callout>

| Midgame farming | Weapons used by your core team | Swapping gear constantly |

| Endgame | Fine-tuning for consistency | Treating attack alone as enough |

<Callout type="success" title="Build Rule That Holds Up">
