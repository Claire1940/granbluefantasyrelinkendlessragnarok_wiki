<Section icon={Swords} title="Skill rotation and combat flow">

The cleanest Maglielle play pattern is simple: enter a safe range, use normal strings to set up pressure, then spend skills only when the boss has committed to an animation or recovery window. That approach makes her feel faster, safer, and more consistent in both solo and co-op content.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Open with safe pressure">
    Start with basic attacks and light positioning. Your first goal is to stay close enough to threaten damage without eating a free punish.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Spend skills on real openings">
    Use active skills when the enemy is locked in place, recovering, stunned, or otherwise unable to punish your commitment.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Reset before greed sets in">
    If your window closes, disengage and rebuild the next opening. Maglielle performs better with repeatable timing than with desperate trades.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Turn burst windows into clean clears">
    Once the boss is controlled, layer your strongest tools and ride the opening into a full damage cycle.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Combat Window | Best Action | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Boss starts a slow attack | Stay close, prepare damage | Safe punish opportunity |
| Boss is stunned | Spend stronger skills | Higher burst output |
| Team triggers Link pressure | Continue offense | Better collective uptime |
| Fight gets chaotic | Reset positioning | Lower risk, cleaner re-entry |

<Callout type="success" title="Execution Tip">
If your damage drops, the problem is usually timing, not raw stats. Rebuild the rotation around better openings before changing the whole loadout.
</Callout>
