<Section icon={Shield} title="Weapons, Sigils, and Mastery Priorities">

The fastest way to improve a Berserker Echo loadout is to stop thinking in isolated upgrades. Weapons, sigils, mastery, and later systems all feed the same damage loop. The official build manual and update pages make that clear by tying character growth to weapon progress, sigil choices, and later systems like Sigil Synthesis and Master Traits.

The biggest mistake is overvaluing attack too early. Once your numbers begin to plateau, the build needs more efficient scaling. That is where Damage Cap, elemental boosts, and premium offensive sigils become more important than another small stat bump.

| Sigil | Best Use | When to Slot | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Damage Cap | Main scaling piece | Mid-game to endgame | Becomes essential once raw attack stops moving numbers enough |
| War Elemental | Premium damage boost | Endgame | Excellent when you already have a stable core build |
| Supplementary Damage | Extra hit value | Endgame | Strong on high-uptime aggressive characters |
| Skill Cooldown | More burst cycles | Early to late game | Helps the build feel smoother and more active |
| HP/Defense | Survival insurance | Early to endgame | Useful when boss patterns start punishing greedy play |

| Weapon Stage | Focus | Spend or Save |
|---|---|---|
| Story phase | Basic upgrades, steady progress | Spend freely on the active main weapon |
| Mid-game | Role-based weapon growth | Save rare materials for your preferred character |
| Post-story | Efficient scaling | Invest harder in your main party only |
| Endless Ragnarok | Master Traits, better endgame shaping | Spend on strong long-term pieces, not filler |
