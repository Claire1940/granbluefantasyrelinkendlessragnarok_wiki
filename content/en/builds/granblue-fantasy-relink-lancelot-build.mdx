<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Best Weapons, Sigils, and Stat Priority">

Lancelot does not need a complicated build tree to feel effective. He needs the right balance of attack scaling, hit consistency, and enough safety to stay in melee range. That makes weapon choice and sigil planning more important than overthinking niche bonuses too early.

The official [character building manual](https://relink.granbluefantasy.jp/en/manual/detail?s=character_building) is a useful cross-check for upgrade systems, while the [Steam store page](https://store.steampowered.com/app/881020/Granblue_Fantasy_Relink/) is helpful for platform and version context.

**Priority table**

| Priority | Pick | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Damage Cap | Fast melee hits need real scaling, not just bigger numbers on paper |
| 2 | Crit and raw attack | Reliable general damage for story, postgame, and co-op |
| 3 | Cooldown help | More skill usage keeps pressure stable |
| 4 | Survival utility | Lets you stay close instead of backing off too often |

**Sigil direction by stage**

| Stage | What to stack | What to delay |
|---|---|---|
| Story and early quests | Attack, health, simple utility | Rare premium sigils |
| Mid game | Crit, cooldown, better damage uptime | Perfect min-max rolls |
| Late game | Damage Cap, strong offensive synergies | Weak filler stats |
| Endgame farming | Premium damage pieces, survival insurance | Unfocused stat stacking |

**Recommended build mix**

| Slot type | Suggested focus | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Weapon | Highest practical attack you can maintain | Keep it upgraded instead of hopping every drop |
| Offense sigil | Damage Cap or crit support | Best long-term scaling |
| Utility sigil | Cooldown or skill comfort | Helps the fast loop stay smooth |
| Defense sigil | HP, mitigation, or revive comfort | Useful when learning hard bosses |

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid this trap">
Do not chase pure attack stats while ignoring real scaling. Once your damage starts hitting ceilings, **Damage Cap** usually matters more than another small attack gain.

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Set Up the Lancelot Loadout">

A strong Lancelot setup should feel like a rhythm, not a spreadsheet. You want one reliable opener, one way to stay on target, and one safety valve for when bosses break your timing. That makes your loadout easier to use in every quest tier.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Pick the fastest weapon you can keep upgraded">
    Start with the best weapon you can reasonably maintain, then raise it as your quest rewards improve. Lancelot benefits more from steady progression than from frequent swapping.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Assign your most reliable damage skills first">
    Put your strongest consistent skills in the slots you use most often. Fast melee characters waste value when their best buttons sit unused.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Add one comfort or mobility option">
    Keep at least one tool that helps you reposition, survive, or re-enter combat quickly. That keeps your uptime high in boss fights.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Test the loop in a real quest">
    Run one normal quest, then adjust. If you are dodging too often or missing openings, trade some greed for more comfort.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Skill slot reference**

| Slot type | What it should do | Ideal use case |
|---|---|---|
| Main damage skill | High uptime, reliable hit value | Boss openings and chain windows |
| Gap closer | Re-enter melee range fast | Moving bosses and stagger recovery |
| Safety skill | Avoid damage or reset positioning | Learning new encounters |
| Flex slot | Damage, utility, or team support | Match the quest you are running |

<Callout type="success" title="Simple rule">
If a skill does not help you stay on target, hit harder, or survive longer, it is probably the wrong slot for your first serious Lancelot build.
</Callout>

</Section>
