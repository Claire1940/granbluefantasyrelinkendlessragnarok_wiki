<Section icon={Target} title="Beatrix Combo Loop and Skill Timing">

The easiest way to understand Beatrix is as a rhythm character. You want to enter the fight cleanly, build momentum with safe attacks, spend your strongest options when the boss is pinned, and then reset before the enemy can punish you.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Open with safe pressure">
    Start with normal strings and short commitments. Your goal is not instant damage spikes; your goal is to read the boss and get into your flow without overextending.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Turn buffs into damage windows">
    Once the fight stabilizes, layer your offensive tools and stay on target. If the boss is moving a lot, hold your stronger actions until the pattern slows down.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Burst during break, stun, or long recovery">
    Spend your best damage tools when the enemy is locked down. That is where Beatrix starts feeling much stronger than a simple melee attacker.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Reset fast and survive the counterattack">
    As soon as the opening ends, reposition. A clean disengage is often worth more than one greedy extra hit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Combat Window | What to Do | Main Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Opening | Light strings, read patterns | Build control |
| Buff window | Commit to stronger actions | Increase output |
| Break/stun | Spend burst tools | Maximize DPS |
| Recovery | Dodge, guard, reposition | Preserve uptime |

| Common Mistake | Cost | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Greedy extra hit | Getting clipped | End strings earlier |
| Burst too early | Lost damage window | Save for stun or break |
| Ignoring spacing | Slower clears | Re-center after each exchange |
| Forgetting reset tools | More deaths | Keep one safety option ready |

<Callout type="tip" title="Combat Tip">
If a boss is too dangerous to fully commit into, play a shorter rotation. A smaller clean loop is usually better than a huge messy one.
</Callout>

</Section>
